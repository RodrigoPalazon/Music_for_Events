Quartet with a Twist

Quartet With A Twist (QWAT) is a string quartet based in Amsterdam, The Netherlands, comprised of conservatory students from around the country. Forming in 2020, the quartet saw a gap in the market for specialised live event and wedding music and, since it’s success from it’s origination in Perth, Western Australia, decided to bring that knowledge across the globe.

The ‘Twist’ in QWAT’s name stands for their unique instrumentation – instead of the standard two violins, viola and cello, the cello has been replaced with a double bass! As a result, QWAT’s sound is unique and sets them apart from other string quartets.

With their fun personalities and high calibre of musicianship, Quartet With A Twist has got you covered for your next event!

Duo Pudica

The idea to put together the two worlds; the Early Music world and the Jazz world meaning not necessarily Jazz itself more the way to approach music from an oral tradition rather than the written approach that governs Early Music.

In this ensemble the repertoire consists of postcolonial Ecuadorian folkloric dances such as the "Pasillos, Albazos and Jumbos", Brazilian Chôros by Pixinguinha, traditional Japanese music for "Koto (floor stringed instrument) and Shakuhachi (flute)" , music from French Baroque composers such as Jean-Philippe Rameau, François Francoeur and music by Johann Sebastian Bach arranged for violin and double bass.

Our project is done in period instruments with gut strings, giving a very colorful and raw sound to our performances that fit very well with the "World Music" and of course a must for historically informed performance practice.

Eudora Quartet

The ensemble Eudora Quartet, based in The Netherlands, is composed of four young string players from Turkey and Spain. The strange year 2020 brought them together to fight for gender equality in music, and to advance their particular vision about repertoire for string quartet.

Throughout music history, female composers have been relegated to a secondary position within society. Even when taking into account their high level of musical education and skill, it was common for women to have their compositions credited to other male artists. Very often women have worked behind the scenes for big names, doing important tasks supporting the careers of a father, a brother, or a husband, while those male artists took all of the public merits. These women were always under wraps, and in many cases, once they were married, their fabulous musical skills became more underrated or simply forgotten.

Eudora Quartet wants to put the work of women where it belongs: in the concert hall. The public needs to listen to these precious pieces of art to begin healing the mistakes and injustices of the past.
